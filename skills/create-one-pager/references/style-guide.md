# One-Pager Style Guide

Rules for writing every section. Apply these in drafts and enforce them in the final pass.

---

## Active Voice

Write the subject first. Make it do the action.

| ❌ Passive | ✅ Active |
|---|---|
| "Progress cannot be tracked by users." | "Users can't track their progress." |
| "Feedback is not surfaced to managers." | "Managers don't see rep feedback." |
| "The feature was requested by CS teams." | "CS teams have asked for this 11 times." |

---

## Short Sentences

One idea per sentence. If a sentence has two clauses joined by "and" or "which" — split it.

❌ Too long:
> "Sales reps struggle to find the right content at the right time, which leads to off-message conversations and longer deal cycles, especially in enterprise accounts where multiple stakeholders are involved."

✅ Broken down:
> Sales reps can't find the right content at the right time. Conversations go off-message. Deal cycles stretch — especially in enterprise accounts with multiple stakeholders.

---

## Bullets Over Dense Prose

Use bullets for:
- Evidence (quotes, ticket counts, churn signals)
- Lists of goals, non-goals, risks
- Milestone details

Use prose for narrative sections — but break it into short paragraphs, not a single dense block:
- Problem narrative: 2–3 paragraphs, 1–2 sentences each
- Solution story: 2–3 paragraphs, 1–2 sentences each
- Why now: 1–2 paragraphs, 1–2 sentences each
- Target Users: 2 paragraphs (primary / secondary)

❌ Dense block:
> Sales reps can't find content during calls. They improvise or send the wrong asset. 17 CS tickets flagged this in Q4. Two enterprise renewals cited it as a blocker.

✅ Short paragraphs:
> Sales reps can't find content during calls. They improvise — or send the wrong asset.
>
> 17 CS tickets flagged this in Q4. Two enterprise renewals cited it as a blocker.

---

## Numbers Beat Adjectives

❌ Vague:
> "Many customers have requested this. There is significant churn risk."

✅ Specific:
> "17 CS tickets in Q4. 3 enterprise accounts cited this as a renewal blocker."

If you don't have numbers, say so honestly: `[data needed — check support tickets]`

---

## Words to Cut

Remove these on final pass:

| Cut this | Replace with |
|---|---|
| leverage | use |
| seamless | (just describe what it does) |
| best-in-class | (cite the metric instead) |
| holistic | (say what's included) |
| robust | (say what it handles) |
| intuitive | (describe the behavior) |
| streamline | (say what step is removed) |
| unlock | (say what becomes possible) |

---

## Handling Unknowns

Never invent content to fill a gap. Use these placeholders:

- `[TBD — needs: customer data from CS]`
- `[TBD — unvalidated assumption, needs 3 customer interviews]`
- `[TBD — eng estimate pending architecture review]`

These are honest and signal follow-up actions — they don't weaken the doc.

---

## Length Targets by Section

| Section | Target length |
|---|---|
| Problem Statement | 3–5 sentences |
| Why Now | 2–4 sentences |
| Proposed Solution | 4–6 sentences |
| Goals / Non-Goals | 2–4 bullets each |
| Target Users | 3–4 sentences |
| Success Metrics | 3 metrics (north star, leading, guardrail) |
| Risks | 2–3 bullets |
| Rough Scope | 3–4 milestone blocks |
| GTM / Validation | 3–4 bullets |

**Total:** 600–1000 words across all sections.
